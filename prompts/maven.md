## AI

### Session 2 Capstone: Latent Demand & AI Product Design

The loop: build → ship → measure → **discover** → redesign

---

#### Latent Demand in the Agent Era (anchor topic)

Latent demand is demand that exists but is suppressed by friction, unfamiliarity, or lack of vocabulary.
When agents drop friction 10x, that demand becomes active — often surprising builders who didn't design for it.

- What latent demand actually is (vs. expressed demand)
- Why agents expand the demand surface: lower friction → new behaviors emerge
- The "adjacent possible" — what becomes viable when cost-to-try collapses?
- Signal types: awkward asks, edge-case workarounds, unexpected usage patterns
- Designing surface area deliberately: what questions can users even *ask* your agent?
- Anti-pattern: building only for expressed demand (what users say in surveys)
- The "no one asked for the iPod" failure mode vs. latent-demand-led roadmaps
- Bridge to Session 2: if you only validate what users can already articulate, you'll miss latent demand entirely

---

#### Behavior Telemetry for AI Products

Traditional analytics (clicks, funnels) miss what matters when AI is in the loop.
You need to track *what users ask*, *how they phrase it*, and *where agents fail or surprise*.

- What to track: query categories, rephrasing attempts, fallback-to-manual, abandonment
- The "awkward ask" taxonomy — unclassifiable requests as a product signal
- Feedback loops: agent logs → product decisions → agent improvements → more data
- The compounding effect: better data → better agent → more usage → more signal
- Anti-pattern: using AI analytics to confirm you built the right thing

---

#### AI-First Product Design Patterns

Most products are designed for human-speed, human-friction. Agent-first design means rethinking what's possible when execution is near-free.

- The 10x friction reduction question: "What would users do if this was instant?"
- Designing for delegation, not just automation — users hand off *judgment*, not just tasks
- Interface patterns for agentic flows: confirmation steps, interrupt points, trust calibration
- When to show the work vs. just show the result
- Scope creep by capability: your agent can do X → users now expect X+1

---

#### The PM Superpower in the Agent Era

AI handles the how. The PM superpower is knowing *what to validate* and *what not to build*.

- AI can answer any question you ask — the trap is asking the wrong questions
- Mapping the validation ladder: assumption → hypothesis → signal → conviction
- Using agents to stress-test your own roadmap ("argue against this feature")
- Anti-pattern: using AI to build confidence instead of to find failure modes
- The uncomfortable question protocol: what would prove you wrong?
- Close: "What would your users do if this was free?" as a product design prompt
